This document ranks 35 concrete mechanisms extracted from 12 competitor systems — the 11 covered in overview.md plus Google's Paper Assistant Tool (PAT) — analyzed 2026-07-13. Method: web research per system to extract each mechanism with repo/paper evidence, then a code-grounded difficulty audit and an adversarial impact review of every mechanism against the current Kapso codebase. Rubric: impact 1-5, difficulty 1-5, priority = 2 × impact − difficulty (ties break toward lower difficulty).

## Qualifying for integration plans

Criterion: top priority band (priority ≥ 4, i.e. 2 × impact − difficulty ≥ 4) with difficulty ≤ 3 — the "impactful, low integration effort" set. The two impact-4 mechanisms that audited to difficulty 4 (ranks 9–10: the Structured EvaluationResult contract and the search-policy/operator separation) are excluded on integration-effort grounds — both cross the control plane (both strategies + orchestrator + stores) — and remain covered by roadmap.md WP1 and WP2 respectively.

The 8 qualifying mechanisms (ranks 1–8; see integration_plans.md for per-mechanism plans):

1. Fail-closed feedback parsing with mechanical artifact validation gate
2. Probabilistic, time-aware parent selection replacing greedy hill-climb
3. Bounded debug/repair operator with multi-draft roots (draft/debug/improve policy)
4. Deterministic scoped history injection: journal / top-k / diverse-inspiration / ancestral / sibling memory scopes
5. Scoped verifier fan-out with complexity-adaptive budgets and grounded synthesis
6. LLM holistic best-node adjudication with deterministic metric fallback
7. Cross-task prior-wisdom store: end-of-run distillation with embedding-threshold prefetch
8. Atomic typed hypothesis/plan contract for proposals
